Biryani recipe



Biryani is a delicious and aromatic rice dish that originates from South Asia, particularly popular in Pakistan. Here's a basic recipe for making chicken biryani:

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 500g chicken, cut into pieces
  • 2 onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh coriander (cilantro)
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh mint leaves
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • Whole spices (1 bay leaf, 4-5 cloves, 4-5 whole peppercorns, 1-inch cinnamon stick)
  • Salt to taste
  • Ghee or oil for cooking
  • Saffron strands soaked in 2 tablespoons of warm milk (optional)
  • Fried onions (for garnish, optional)
  • Fried cashews and raisins (for garnish, optional)

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the Chicken:
    • In a bowl, mix the chicken pieces with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Prepare the Rice:
    • Wash the basmati rice thoroughly under running water until the water runs clear. Soak the rice in water for about 30 minutes.
    • Bring a large pot of water to boil. Add salt and whole spices (bay leaf, cloves, peppercorns, cinnamon stick). Add the soaked and drained rice to the boiling water.
    • Cook the rice until it is 70-80% done (parboiled). Drain the rice and set aside.
  3. Cook the Chicken Masala:
    • Heat ghee or oil in a large, heavy-bottomed pot over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
    • Add sliced onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
    • Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn soft and oil starts to separate.
    • Add marinated chicken pieces along with the marinade. Cook on medium-high heat until the chicken is almost cooked through and the masala is well blended.
    • Add green chilies, chopped coriander, and mint leaves. Mix well and adjust salt and spices according to your taste.
    • Remove half of this chicken masala and set aside.
  4. Layering the Biryani:
    • In the pot with the remaining chicken masala, spread half of the parboiled rice evenly.
    • Sprinkle half of the garam masala powder and drizzle half of the saffron milk (if using).
    • Spread the reserved chicken masala over the rice.
    • Layer the remaining rice on top and sprinkle the remaining garam masala and saffron milk.
    • Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid. You can also seal the edges of the lid with a dough made of flour to trap the steam (optional).
  5. Dum Cooking (Slow Cooking):
    • Place the pot on very low heat (you can place a tava or griddle under the pot to diffuse heat) and cook for 20-25 minutes. This slow cooking process is known as "dum."
    • Alternatively, you can bake the biryani in a preheated oven at 180°C (350°F) for 20-25 minutes.
  6. Serve:
    • Once done, gently fluff the biryani with a fork.
    • Garnish with fried onions, fried cashews, and raisins if desired.
    • Serve hot with raita (yogurt salad) and/or your favorite side dish.
