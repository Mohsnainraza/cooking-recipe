Mutton karahi recipe

Mutton Karahi, like its chicken counterpart, is a flavorful and robust dish originating from Pakistan and North India. It's cooked in a karahi (wok-like pan) which enhances the flavors through slow cooking and simmering. Here's a traditional recipe for Mutton Karahi:

Ingredients

  • 750g mutton (goat meat), cut into medium-sized pieces
  • 3 tablespoons cooking oil or ghee
  • 2 large onions, finely sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit (adjust according to spice preference)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander (cilantro) leaves, chopped, for garnish
  • Fresh ginger, julienned, for garnish (optional)
  • Lemon wedges, for serving

Instructions

  1. Heat the Karahi:
    • Heat oil or ghee in a karahi or heavy-bottomed pan over medium heat.
  2. Sauté Onions:
    • Add sliced onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
  3. Add Ginger-Garlic Paste:
    • Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for 1-2 minutes until the raw smell disappears.
  4. Cook Tomatoes:
    • Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and oil starts to separate from the masala.
  5. Add Spices:
    • Add cumin seeds, coriander powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well and cook for another minute.
  6. Add Mutton:
    • Add the mutton pieces to the karahi. Increase the heat to medium-high and stir-fry the mutton for 5-7 minutes until it changes color and is sealed.
  7. Cook Covered:
    • Cover the karahi with a lid and cook the mutton on medium heat for about 1.5 to 2 hours or until the mutton is tender, stirring occasionally. Add a little water if needed during cooking to prevent sticking and to achieve desired consistency.
  8. Finish with Garam Masala:
    • Once the mutton is cooked and tender, sprinkle garam masala powder over it and mix well. Cook for another 1-2 minutes.
  9. Garnish and Serve:
    • Garnish with chopped fresh coriander leaves and optionally with julienned ginger.
    • Serve hot with naan bread, roti, or rice, and lemon wedges on the side.
