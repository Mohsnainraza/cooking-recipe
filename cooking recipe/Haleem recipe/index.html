Haleem recipe

Haleem is a hearty and wholesome stew popular in South Asian cuisine, particularly in Pakistan. It's made from a combination of meat (usually beef or chicken), lentils, and wheat, simmered together to create a thick, porridge-like texture. Here's a traditional recipe for making Chicken

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup broken wheat (dalia)
  • 1/2 cup split Bengal gram (chana dal)
  • 1/4 cup split red lentils (masoor dal)
  • 500g boneless chicken, cut into small pieces
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 cup cooking oil or ghee
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander (cilantro) leaves, chopped, for garnish
  • Fried onions, for garnish (optional)
  • Lemon wedges, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Preparation:
    • Wash the broken wheat, split Bengal gram, and split red lentils thoroughly under running water. Soak them in water separately for at least 1 hour.
  2. Cooking the Lentils and Wheat:
    • In a large pot, combine the soaked broken wheat, split Bengal gram, and split red lentils with about 6 cups of water.
    • Bring to a boil over medium-high heat, then reduce the heat to low and simmer, partially covered, until the lentils and wheat are very tender and well cooked (about 1.5 to 2 hours). Stir occasionally and add more water if necessary to prevent sticking and ensure it doesn't dry out. The mixture should be very soft and mushy..
  3. Prepare the Chicken:
    • While the lentils and wheat are cooking, heat oil or ghee in a separate pan over medium heat.
    • Add sliced onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
    • Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for another minute until fragrant.
    • Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften and oil starts to separate.
    • Add chicken pieces, turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala powder, black pepper, cinnamon, cloves, nutmeg, and salt. Mix well and cook until the chicken is cooked through and tender.
  4. Combine Chicken with Lentil-Wheat Mixture:
    • Once the lentils and wheat are very soft and mushy, use a hand blender or mash them well with a potato masher to achieve a smooth texture. You can leave some texture if desired..
    • Add the cooked chicken mixture to the pot of lentils and wheat. Mix well to combine everything thoroughly.
  5. Simmering Haleem:
    • Place the pot back on low heat and simmer the haleem, stirring frequently, for another 30-40 minutes. This helps to meld the flavors together and achieve a thicker consistency. Add more water if necessary to reach your desired thickness.
  6. Serve:
    • Once the haleem reaches the desired consistency, check for seasoning and adjust salt and spices if needed..
    • Serve hot, garnished with chopped fresh coriander (cilantro), fried onions (if using), and lemon wedges on the side..
    • Haleem is traditionally enjoyed with naan bread or parathas.
